Scholarship grants for PSL master programs

PSL Research Master program applicants are eligible for a monthly scholarship of €1 200 for 10 months (September to June). (Registration fees are covered by the scholarship and remain to be paid by the applicant in due time.)

In addition, the PR[AI]RIE institute provides extra grants for master programs specifically in Artificial Intelligence (M1 I2D, M2 IASD only). Note: for M1 I2D applicants, a commitment to studying Artificial Intelligence should be included in the motivation letter.

Who can apply?

This scheme, based on academic excellence, is open to students of all nationalities who have obtained a bachelor's degree (for enrolment in Master 1) or having validated a Master 1 year (for enrolment in Master 2), or equivalent degrees.

The grant will only be awarded to applicants who have registered to one of the track of the Master program in computer science of PSL.

  • 1st year of master in computer science, specialty Computer Science for Decision (I2D)
  • 2nd year of master in computer science, specialty Artificial Intelligence, Systems, Data (IASD)
  • 2nd year of master in computer science, specialty Modelling, Optimisation, Decision and Organisation (MODO)
  • 2nd year of master in computer science, specialty Algorithmics and foundations of programming (MPRI) conditioned to registering to the master in PSL via the ENS (only for students registered in M1 at PSL).

Important notes:

  • Interruption of attendance is likely to result in the immediate termination of the scholarship payments in accordance with the university’s rules and regulations.
  • These grants cannot be combined with other types of funding (with the exception of joint programmes with French Embassies or French Institutes abroad).

How to apply?

  • For the 2nd year master programs M2 IASD and M2 MODO, apply to the master program using the university platform MyCandidature, and tick the corresponding checkbox during the application procedure. Then add as supplementary material, a short motivation letter explaining why you deserve this grant and how it will allow you to continue your studies in good conditions.
  • For the 2nd year master program M2 MPRI, send a short motivation letter explaining why you deserve this grant and how it will allow you to continue your studies in good conditions to the director of the Graduate Programme. Warning: You can only apply if you have graduated from a M1 Master program from PSL.
  • For the first year master program M1 I2D, the application procedure depends on whether you apply to the master via the national platform MonMaster or not. (Note: application via MonMaster is required for most EU students and other international students who have access to the Études en France program. More details about who is required to apply via MonMaster here.)
    • If you apply to the master via MonMaster, you must make another application on the University platform MyCandidature specially for the grant. Follow this link, and apply to the Computer Science Graduate Program -- scholarship of excellence campaign.
    • If you do not need to apply via MonMaster, simply tick the corresponding checkbox during the MyCandidature application, and add as supplementary material, a short motivation letter explaining why you deserve this grant and how it will allow you to continue your studies in good conditions.
The grant application must be completed before the end of the first session of Master applications for M1 IDD, M2 IASD, M2 MODO and at the latest on 13 June for M2 MPRI.
